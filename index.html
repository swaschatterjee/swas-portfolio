The Challenge

The North America COO identified concerning patterns of revenue loss across multiple programs, but the root causes and scale were unclear. Revenue was slipping through operational cracks, and without systematic controls, the problem would continue to grow.

My Approach

  • Cross-functional investigation: Led discovery sessions with Product, Operations, Finance, and Engineering to map end-to-end revenue flows
  • Data-driven analysis: Analyzed claims data, billing cycles, and service fulfillment patterns to identify systemic gaps
  • Root cause identification: Uncovered process bottlenecks in claims adjudication, billing timing mismatches, and service delivery tracking gaps
  • Portfolio-wide solution design: Designed repeatable process controls and automation triggers that could scale across all programs

Actions Taken

  • Implemented automated reconciliation checkpoints between service delivery and billing systems
  • Established weekly revenue assurance reviews with Operations and Finance
  • Created dashboards to provide real-time visibility into revenue flow anomalies
  • Developed standard operating procedures that were rolled out across the entire portfolio
  • Trained operations teams on the new controls and monitoring processes
Impact: Recovered ~$500K in revenue leakage and established repeatable controls that prevented future loss across the entire portfolio. The framework became a standard part of program governance for all new initiatives.

ðŸ¤– Case Study 2: AI/ML Enablement Across Operations

The Challenge

Claims processing, call center operations, and service delivery were heavily manual, creating bottlenecks, delays, and high operational costs. The organization needed to modernize operations while maintaining service quality and compliance standards.

My Approach

  • Opportunity assessment: Partnered with Product, Engineering, and Operations to identify high-impact automation opportunities
  • Use case prioritization: Evaluated potential AI/ML applications based on ROI, implementation complexity, and operational impact
  • Pilot program design: Structured phased rollouts to test, learn, and refine before full deployment
  • Change management: Worked closely with operations teams to ensure adoption and address concerns about automation

Key Initiatives Delivered

  • Claims adjudication automation: Implemented ML models to auto-approve straightforward claims, reducing manual review time by ~40%
  • Call center AI: Deployed natural language processing for tier-1 support, deflecting ~25% of routine inquiries
  • Service delivery optimization: Used predictive analytics to optimize technician routing and parts inventory
  • Quality assurance automation: Built automated testing frameworks to reduce manual QA effort and improve coverage
Impact: Improved operational efficiency by ~15%, saving ~$400K annually in claim processing and support costs. Reduced backlog by 30% and improved customer satisfaction scores by enabling faster response times.

The Challenge
